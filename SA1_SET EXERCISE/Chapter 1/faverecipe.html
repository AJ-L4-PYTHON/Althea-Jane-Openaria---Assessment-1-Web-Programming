Chicken Cordon Bleu with Dijon Cream Sauce


Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 25 to 35 Minutes
Total Time: 45 to 55 Minutes
Servings: 4 Servings



Chicken Cordon Bleu is a classic dish consisting of chicken breast wrapped around ham and Swiss cheese, then breaded and fried or baked. The name "Cordon Bleu" is French, meaning "blue ribbon," and originally referred to a prestigious order of knighthood. The dish itself, however, is not of French origin but rather Swiss. It's believed to have been inspired by the traditional Swiss dish "veal cordon bleu," which consists of veal wrapped around ham and cheese. Over time, the chicken version gained popularity and became a staple of French and international cuisine, often served with a creamy sauce. This chicken dish is everything you want and more: Tender chicken, ham, and swiss cheese rolled and lightly breaded, then baked to crispy perfection and topped with a rich dijon cream sauce! Ready in just minutes!


Ingredients

Chicken Cordon Bleu

  • 4 boneless skinless chicken breasts - pounded to even ¼-½ inch thickness
  • 4 slices ham
  • 4 slices swiss cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk - or water
  • ⅓ cup Italian seasoned breadcrumbs - (or regular breadcrumbs + 1 ½ teaspoons Italian seasoning)
  • ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese - see note
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ⅓ cup shredded parmesan cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Procedure

Prepare the chicken

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. In a medium bowl, stir together parmesan cheese and breadcrumbs. Set aside.
  2. In a second, small bowl, whisk together eggs and milk. Set aside.
  3. Lay pounded chicken breasts out on a flat, clean surface. Top each with a slice of ham and then a slice of swiss cheese.
  4. Roll into a bundle starting with one of the longer sides and rolling toward the opposite side. Secure with toothpicks.
  5. Dip chicken bundles in the egg mixture, coating all sides, then carefully roll in breadcrumbs to coat.
  6. Melt butter in an oven-safe skillet over medium high heat. (See note if you don't have an oven-safe skillet)
  7. Brown chicken for 2-3 minutes on each side until browned all over.
  8. Transfer skillet to preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes until chicken is cooked through.

Prepare the sauce

  1. While chicken is cooking begin preparing the sauce by melting butter in a medium sauce pan.
  2. Stir in flour until mixture clumps together. Gradually whisk in milk, working out the lumps as you stir. Stir in dijon mustard, garlic powder, parmesan cheese, and salt and pepper to taste.
  3. When chicken bundles are fully cooked, cut into 1-inch sections, drizzle with the sauce and serve immediately.

Note

For the parmesan cheese: I highly recommend freshly grated parmesan cheese for the sauce! The kind that comes in a shaker can or comes pre-shredded in a bag or container, does not melt the same way that freshly grated parmesan does. For the chicken breading, grated parmesan from the can is my favorite as it yields a really deliciously crispy crust, but if you want to use just one kind of cheese, freshly grated will work here as well!

Oven-safe skillet alternate option: If you don’t have an oven-safe skillet, no worries! Simply brown your chicken bundles in any skillet, then transfer to a greased casserole/baking dish before baking.
